Wrap Media

A quick introduction to the Wrap platform — a Wrap is a highly-focused, app-like, mobile web experience.

Wraps are mobile web apps and live at a URL. The flexibility of the URL allows a end user to enter the Wrap through many channels including social feeds, e-mail, web advertisement or, like shown below, through SMS.

The Minted Challange Wrap seen above I worked on with Jeff Klein— He worked the visual design while I focused on the interactions.

Wrap experiences are essentially a colleciton of cards — a new "page" of the traditional "app" is analagous to a card. The creation and distribution of the experiences was handled through a SASS application that we developed in-house as well.

The way that myself and the design team designed wraps was by designing cards. From the example above you can see that these experiences follow and "x-cross" pattern — The end user is able to scroll either up/down or swipe left or right.

I designed and developed mobile-first web interactions for our product and external clients.

Wrap's Studio Design Team partnered with Warner Bros. Records to create a mobile-first merchandise catalog for indie pop duo, Tegan & Sara.

Myself with Wrap's Studio Design Team and Warner Bros. Records partnered to create a mobile-first merchandise catalog for the Tegan & Sara's “Love You 2 Death” tour.

Working closely with the coolest visual designer around, Theo Arguna, I designed and developed the "Commerce" and "Share" interactions shown below.

Our approach was an immersive one, to value interaction and interface but not more than the content itself. We helped Tegan & Sara create a brand based on two basic elements; their highly curated content and the enthusiastic followers that make up their team. We avoided gimmicks by focusing on their curators and content.

Nearby Services Component for our self service small business customers

As one of our self-service product offerings we built an integration with the Google Places API's for helping users find the nearest location of that service near them. For the case below the customer was a small gym business and a user could find a branch local to their location.

This component was a "self serve" part of our product. Anyone with an account could add this to a card. I worked on the motion and interaction here and I worked directly with the Google API's while developing this.
